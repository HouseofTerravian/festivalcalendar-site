ADA Accessibility Statement

Last updated: May 2026

FestivalCalendar.online is committed to providing an accessible and inclusive digital experience for all users, including people with disabilities.

Our Commitment

We strive to follow accessibility best practices and aim to meet the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA standards. Our ongoing efforts include:

  • Readable text with sufficient contrast ratios
  • Clear, logical page navigation and structure
  • Mobile-friendly, responsive layout
  • Keyboard-navigable interface design
  • Descriptive alt text for images where applicable
  • Accessible form labels and error messaging
  • Proper semantic HTML structure
  • ARIA labels on interactive elements

Known Limitations

FestivalCalendar.online is an evolving platform. Some features or third-party content embedded on the site may not fully meet accessibility standards at this time. We are actively working to identify and resolve these gaps.

Feedback & Assistance

If you experience difficulty accessing any part of FestivalCalendar.online, or if you need information in an alternative format, please contact us:

  • Email: support@festivalcalendar.online

We will make reasonable efforts to improve accessibility and to assist users who encounter barriers on our site. Please include a description of the page or feature you had difficulty with and your preferred contact method so we can respond appropriately.

Third-Party Content

FestivalCalendar.online may link to or embed content from third-party providers such as ticketing platforms, payment processors, and external websites. We cannot guarantee the accessibility of third-party content and encourage users to report any issues they encounter.

Ongoing Improvement

We are committed to continuously improving the accessibility of our platform. This statement will be updated as we make improvements and as accessibility standards evolve.
